YouTube

This August 2023, people started seeing enhanced bitrate options in some videos. Youtube says that it will not mess with the old 1080p, but many people started seeing differences.

Is there actually no difference? Were we just getting used to the 4K options that the 1080p looks bad now, or is it just a marketing strategy to get more people to subscribe to premium?

Twitter to X

On October 2022, Elon Musk acquired Twitter. He rebranded it to X on July 2023.

He claims he is aiming for an everything apps that delivers user true freedom, however, he started involving money into the app.

As of April 1, 2023, users have to subscribe for $8 a month to get the blue tick. It has it perks, but I believe that the freedom Mr. Musk speaks of is for the rich.

Streaming services are getting more expensive.

We know inflation is getting crazy, but is it necessary for these digital subscription-based services to hike up the prices? Are the corporates increasing the people's salary or are they keeping it to themselves? Where does that leave us?

The competition going on between these corporates are not good for the consumers. Do we have to subscribe for every single platforms just to watch one of their original movie?

Thinking about it more carefully, these digital subscriptions only lasts to the corporates desires. 5 or 20 years from now, we might want to watch a movie, but we wouldn't have access to it even though we paid for it back then.

We are becoming more and more enslaved to these greedy corporates.
